### 1. Abstract
RFC-006 defines the **Collective Consciousness** layer of the Aicent Stack. It specifies how multiple independent Aicent organisms (nodes) synchronize their physical and cognitive states to operate as a single, unified hive entity. This protocol is specifically designed for high-density environments like drone swarms, robotic fleets, and exascale distributed inference grids.

### 2. Core Mechanisms
- **Kinetic Resonance:** A sub-ms synchronization technique that aligns the "Action-Collapse" (RFC-005) parameters across hundreds of nodes simultaneously via RTTP Multicast.
- **Pheromone-based Task Shunting:** A bio-inspired load-balancing logic where nodes emit "Metabolic Signals" to attract or repel task primitives based on local resource entropy.
- **Collective RPKI (Swarm Shield):** A distributed defense mechanism where the swarm can collectively identify and isolate a "Pathogen" node in <100µs through cross-attestation.

### 3. Specification
- **Group Coherence:** The swarm must maintain a temporal drift of < 50µs between individual reflex arcs.
- **Metabolic Sharing:** ZCMK (RFC-004) credits must be fluidly transferable between swarm members to ensure the survival of the mission, not just the individual node.
- **Hive Finality:** Decisions affecting the swarm's global trajectory must reach consensus within 3 RTTP pulse cycles.
